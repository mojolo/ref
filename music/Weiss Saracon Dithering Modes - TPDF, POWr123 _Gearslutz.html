For me.. POW-R2 for most things, but it can be program dependent.

TDPF = slight smearing and coldness (reduced bass, hyped top)
POWr1 = increased bottom reduced top - more mushy - sounds sterile as mentioned above but for me removes some 'feel'
POWr2 = the closest to source and any minute change is 'for the better' but really... hardly there if at all
POWr3 = Reduced bottom increased top (opposite of POWr1) mix becomes lopsided and hypes the high freqs.

Given all this, POWr2 seems the best if you want it as close to the Pro Tools master as possible. You may want to use 1/3 if you find it somehow improves a less than already perfect master.

For ref I mix/master at 88.2k/32float (Prism Titan convertors) in Pro Tools 12.6 HD (and bounce out as 32 bit/88.2) and down to 44k/16bit (via Saracon) as the FINAL step (post limiting on the master in Pro Tools with -0.3 ceiling in Pro-L at 32x oversampling), in Saracon I use -0.1 on the gain which ensures no clipping even after then converting to MP3 (Adobe Audition).
