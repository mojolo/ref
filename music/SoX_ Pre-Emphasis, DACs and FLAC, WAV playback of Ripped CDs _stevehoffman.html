    Your CD transport needs to be able to decode pre-emphasis - not all of them do, because pre-emphasis isn't really used much anymore. Some players will decode it before passing through an external DAC.

    Ripping to FLAC or WAV, you need a cuesheet that lists the pre-emphasis flags that tell the software or device what to do, and that software or device needs to support it.

    Hawkmoon said:
    I was left wondering - if you run your CD transport into an external DAC, does the DAC correctly apply the de-emphasis filter to relevant CDs?
    Click to expand...
    It depends 100% on the nature of the external DAC and the CDP/Transport. I had one DAC that DID NOT apply PE. The DAC I have now does apply PE.

    You can test this yourself with a CDP by hooking up the analog outputs and the DAC via coax digital to the CDP. You can toggle between the inputs on your amp very quickly and the difference should be immediately noticeable. If the PE is not applied it will sound tinnier than your CDP, assuming the CDP can apply PE on its own. Some newer CDPs removed this feature, but not all.

    For ripping CDs I use Foobar plug-ins to apply PE after ripping the disc.
