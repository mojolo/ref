I was asked about the quality of the EZ CD Audio Converter's DSD to PCM and PCM to DSD converters (especially against dbPoweramp).

I already knew the DSD quality in EZ CD Audio Converter is very good because a lot of audio quality optimizations and comparisons have been done. But I made a little test. I followed the same procedure that was used in Archimago's Musings blogs' DSD encoder & decoder analysis.

Procedure:
- Create 24-bit 96 kHz PCM test signal with RightMark Audio Analyzer
- Convert 24-bit 96 kHz PCM test signal to DSD64 with different PCM->DSD converters
- Convert back the DSD64 test signal to 24-bit 96 kHz with different DSD->PCM converters
- Analyze the results RightMark Audio Analyzer

DSD Converter softwares tested:
dbPoweramp 17.1
EZ CD Audio Converter 9.1.6
Weiss Saracon 1.6.1-27 (the "Professional" tool)

dbPoweramp settings:
PCM->DSD: Default settings (no other settings available)
DSD->PCM: Default settings (no other settings available)

EZ CD Audio Convert settings:
PCM->DSD: Default settings (no other settings available)
DSD->PCM: Ultra High Quality (default: Very High Quality)
